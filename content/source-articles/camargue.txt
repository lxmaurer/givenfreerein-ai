TITLE: Camargue Horses

STANDFIRST: The white horses of southern France live in one of Europe's most distinctive wetland landscapes.

BYLINE: Alex Maurer

BODY:
The Camargue horse is one of the oldest breeds in Europe.

These horses live in the wetlands of southern France where they have adapted to marshland, salt air, and seasonal flooding.

For centuries they have worked alongside the Gardians, the mounted cattle herders of the Camargue region.

Today the horses remain a living symbol of the region's culture and landscape.
